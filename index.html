About the PRECAST Handbook

The PRECAST Handbook is a clinical reference resource for health professionals involved in cancer surgery prehabilitation programs. It provides evidence-based, structured guidance on the medical optimisation of patients undergoing major cancer surgery.

Content is drawn from the Prep-4-Cancer Surgery Toolkit, developed by Peter MacCallum Cancer Centre in collaboration with Western & Central Melbourne Integrated Cancer Services (WCMICS).

🎯

Target Audience

Surgeons, anaesthetists, nurses, allied health professionals, and general practitioners in cancer care.

📚

Evidence-Based

All chapters are grounded in current clinical evidence, consensus guidelines, and best practice recommendations.

🔄

Living Document

Chapters are updated regularly as new evidence emerges. New chapters are added as the handbook expands.

How to Add a New Chapter

The handbook is designed to be easily extended. Follow the steps below to add a new chapter.

  1. 1

    Prepare your chapter content

    Write your chapter in Markdown format following the PRECAST chapter structure (Overview → Learning Objectives → Key Clinical Recommendations → Sections → Clinical Decision Pathway → References).

  2. 2

    Create a new HTML page

    Copy anaemia.html or diabetes.html as a template and rename it (e.g., cardiac.html). Replace the content sections with your new chapter content.

  3. 3

    Update the Table of Contents

    Open index.html and add a new chapter card in the Table of Contents section, following the same pattern as the Anaemia or Diabetes cards. Update the chapter number, title, description, and link.

  4. 4

    Update the navigation in all pages

    Add a link to the new chapter in the sidebar navigation of each existing chapter page so readers can move between chapters easily.

  5. 5

    Deploy the updated site

    Re-deploy the handbook website folder to publish the changes. The live URL will remain the same.

💡 Naming Conventions

  • Use lowercase, hyphen-separated filenames: cardiac-optimisation.html
  • Chapter numbers are sequential: next chapter is Chapter 11
  • Keep the README.md updated with a list of all chapters
